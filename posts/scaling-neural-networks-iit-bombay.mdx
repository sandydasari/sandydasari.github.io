During my time at IIT Bombay I worked on neural network optimization and scaling. A few things I expected from papers turned out to be true; a few did not.

## What held up

- **Learning-rate schedules matter more than architecture tweaks** at moderate scale.
- **Batch size is a regularizer**, and the "linear scaling rule" is a reasonable starting point but rarely the final answer.
- Mixed precision is essentially free once you stop fighting NaNs.

## What surprised me

- The gap between "trains" and "trains well" is wider than the literature suggests. Two runs with identical hyperparameters can diverge late in training based on data ordering alone.
- Implicit regularization from SGD is real but fragile. Switching optimizers mid-run usually punishes you.

## A practical heuristic

If you're scaling a model and unsure where to invest engineering time, instrument loss curves at multiple resolutions before changing anything. Most "we need a bigger model" decisions reverse once you can see what's actually happening in the first 5% of training.
